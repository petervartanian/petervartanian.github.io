      <div class="pv-fj-below">
        <p>Fas/Jus is a juridical-intelligence and R+D+I studio — whose purpose is to answer the defining question of twenty-first-century statecraft: &#8220;What is the gray zone?&#8221;</p>
        <p>In Roman jurisprudence, <em>fas</em> and <em>jus</em> are not synonyms. <em>Fas</em> is the unwritten, cosmic layer of what is permitted by the order of things, prior to statute and prior to state. <em>Jus</em> is positive, constructed law — the rights and obligations that states write down and enforce. The gap between them is the oldest problem in jurisprudence. It is also, precisely, the definition of the gray zone.</p>
        <p>Fas/Jus is founded on the proposition that twenty-first-century statecraft has migrated almost entirely into that gap — the space where legality and legitimacy diverge, where attribution is deliberately muddied, and where the actual instruments of power are export controls, sanctions regimes, supply-chain chokepoints, and norm contestation rather than armies. The studio is the attempt to map it.</p>
        <p class="pv-fj-note">Fortune 500 companies, scholar-practitioners, and public institutions. All engagements are confidential.</p>

        <p class="pv-fj-panel-hed">Fas builds the architecture before the line is drawn.</p>
        <p>Its clients are constructing something: a policy, an institution, a legal instrument, or a strategic framework for operating in contested normative terrain. Fas answers the question <em>what should this look like</em> — before the statute is written, before the instrument is designed, before the border is placed.</p>
        <p>It operates at the level of the unwritten. In Roman jurisprudence, <em>fas</em> was the cosmic layer of permission — prior to state, prior to code, prior to enforcement. Fas's work belongs to that register: first-principles juridical inquiry translated into frameworks that are designed to survive contact with hard cases.</p>
        <p>Its engagements are by invitation, and its outputs belong to the client and are governed by confidentiality agreements.</p>

It reads the law as terrain and reports on movement across it.</p>
